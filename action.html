Taking Action for Sustainable Technology

Comparing unsustainable and sustainable choices and creating a school action plan for change.

Unsustainable vs Sustainable Gadgets

Unsustainable Gadget

  • Uses minerals from unregulated mines
  • Produced in factories with low wages
  • Short lifespan and difficult to repair
  • Creates large amounts of electronic waste

Sustainable Gadget

  • Uses ethically sourced and recycled materials
  • Fair wages and safe working conditions
  • Designed for repair and long-term use
  • Reduced environmental impact

Sustainable Alternatives

One sustainable alternative is to purchase devices from companies that focus on ethical sourcing and fair labour practices, such as Fairphone. Another alternative is to repair old devices instead of replacing them frequently. Recycling electronic waste also reduces environmental harm.

Multiple Perspectives

  • Workers: Benefit from fair wages and safe working conditions.
  • Consumers: Make responsible choices that support ethical companies.
  • Environment: Protected from pollution and excessive mining.
  • Companies: Improve reputation by using sustainable practices.

School Action Plan

The following actions can be implemented in school to promote ethical consumption:

  • Fair Trade Fridays: Encourage students to learn about fair trade and ethical products.
  • Ethical Canteen Choices: Promote environmentally friendly and responsibly sourced items.
  • Awareness Week: Organize presentations and posters on the hidden costs of gadgets.
  • E-Waste Collection Drive: Set up bins for safe disposal and recycling of old electronics.

Digital Campaign

This website acts as a digital campaign tool to raise awareness about global trade and the hidden costs of gadgets. It provides information, visuals, and action steps for students to understand their role in creating a more sustainable future.
