Product Journey of a Gadget

Tracing the journey of a gadget from raw material extraction to the consumer market.

Trade Journey Overview

A gadget passes through several stages before reaching the consumer. These include the extraction of raw materials, manufacturing in factories, and final distribution to retail stores.

1. Raw Materials

Minerals such as cobalt, lithium, copper, and gold are mined from the Earth. These materials are essential for batteries, circuit boards, and screens. Many mines are located in developing countries where safety regulations may be weak.

2. Manufacturing

Raw materials are transported to factories where components are produced and assembled into finished gadgets. Workers may face long working hours, low wages, and unsafe working conditions.

3. Distribution and Sales

Finished gadgets are shipped worldwide to retail stores and online markets. Transportation contributes to carbon emissions and environmental pollution.

Journey Flowchart

The journey of a gadget can be summarized as follows:

  • Mining of raw materials in countries such as the Democratic Republic of Congo and Chile
  • Manufacturing in factories in China, Vietnam, and other industrial regions
  • Distribution to global markets including Europe, Asia, and North America

This global movement of goods shows how trade connects producers and consumers across continents.

Multiple Perspectives

  • Miners: Work in difficult and sometimes dangerous conditions to extract minerals.
  • Factory Workers: Assemble products under pressure to meet global demand.
  • Consumers: Benefit from technology but are often unaware of its production impacts.
  • Environment: Suffers from mining damage, pollution, and electronic waste.
