Unmasking the Hidden Costs of Gadgets

An investigation into the global trade journey of electronic devices and their social and environmental impacts.

Global Trade and Technology

Introduction

Gadgets such as smartphones and laptops are used daily by millions of people. However, behind every device lies a complex global trade system involving mining, manufacturing, and transportation. This website explores the hidden costs of gadget production, including child labour, environmental damage, and low wages.

Product Journey

Raw Materials

Minerals such as cobalt and lithium are extracted from the Earth for use in batteries and circuits.

Factories

Workers assemble gadgets in factories, often under difficult conditions and low wages.

Stores

Finished products are sold to consumers in stores and online across the world.

Hidden Costs

  • Child labour in mining industries
  • Deforestation and water pollution
  • Unsafe working conditions in factories
  • Electronic waste affecting human health

What Can We Do?

Consumers can support sustainable technology companies, recycle electronic waste, and promote ethical production. Schools can organize awareness programs such as Fair Trade Fridays and environmental campaigns.
